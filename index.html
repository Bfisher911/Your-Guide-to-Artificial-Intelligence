What is AI? The Iceberg Analogy

Artificial Intelligence is the science of creating machines that can perform tasks requiring human intelligence, like learning and problem-solving. But what we interact with is just the tip of the iceberg.

The Tip: The intuitive user interface: chatbots, voice assistants, and recommendation feeds.
The Depths: The vast hidden machinery: massive datasets, complex algorithms, data center infrastructure, and human labor for labeling and refinement.

Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI)

The Specialist. This is the only AI we have today. It's designed for a single task, like playing chess or recommending movies, and it does it exceptionally well. All current AI applications are ANI.

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

The All-Rounder. A hypothetical AI with human-like intelligence, capable of understanding, learning, and applying knowledge across diverse tasks. AGI does not yet exist and remains theoretical.

Artificial Superintelligence (ASI)

The Oracle. A theoretical intellect vastly smarter than the best human minds in every field. ASI is a concept that drives long-term safety research and philosophical debate.

The Human in the Loop

AI is a powerful tool, but it's not an infallible oracle. It can "hallucinate" or reproduce biases from its training data. Your critical thinking, domain expertise, and ethical judgment are the most important apps. Your role is to guide, question, verify, and correct.

AI automates tasks. You provide the wisdom.
